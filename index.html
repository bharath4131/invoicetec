Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about InvoiceFlow? Here are answers to the most common queries.

Is InvoiceFlow completely free to use? ▼
Yes! InvoiceFlow is a 100% free tool. There are no monthly subscriptions, paywalls, or limits on the number of invoices or customers you can manage.
Where is my business data stored? ▼
To prioritize your privacy, we store all database records locally in your browser's IndexedDB. Your invoice history, client listings, and company profiles are never sent to our servers. Your financial data stays entirely under your control.
Can I backup or transfer my data? ▼
Absolutely. Under the Settings page, we provide full Export and Import capabilities. You can download your entire database as a single JSON file at any time and import it on another device or browser.
Does the tool support multiple currencies? ▼
Yes, you can configure your company's default currency and customize the currency for each invoice individually. We support USD ($), EUR (€), GBP (£), INR (₹), CAD (C$), AUD (A$), JPY (¥), and SGD (S$).

About InvoiceFlow

Learn about the philosophy, architecture, and team behind the platform.

Our Mission: Privacy First Invoicing

InvoiceFlow was founded with a simple belief: billing tools shouldn't harvest your sensitive business data. Traditional online invoice builders require you to upload your client lists, rate sheets, and revenue details to their servers. We designed a modern, local-first alternative.

By using modern browser technologies like Dexie.js (IndexedDB) and client-side PDF compilers (pdfmake), we ensure all compute and database storage occurs entirely on your device. This architecture provides near-instant loading times, offline usability, and absolute privacy for your business operations.

📧 One-Click Email Sending (EmailJS)

Configure EmailJS credentials to email your invoices directly to customers from this app.

How to set up your EmailJS Template
  1. Log in to your EmailJS dashboard and create a new email template.
  2. In the template editor, use the parameter {{client_name}} for the customer, {{invoice_number}} for the number, and {{to_email}} for the target address.
  3. Go to the **Attachments** tab or add an attachment with the path/variable {{invoice_pdf}} to automatically attach the PDF file.
